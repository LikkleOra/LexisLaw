# The AntiGravity Pro-Level Blueprint

**Stop "Guessing" and Start Building Real Products**

Most people get average results because they give average instructions. If you want to build an app that actually works (and looks amazing), you need a system.

Follow this **4-Step Pro Workflow** to go from "Idea" to "Live App" in record time.

---

### 🧱 Step 1: The "Painful Specificity" Phase

**Never start building inside AntiGravity.** Start in a chat (like Claude) to sharpen the logic.

- **The Amateur Move:** "Build me a fitness app."
- **The Pro Move:** "Build a HIIT timer app for garage gym owners that tracks personal records, includes a rest-day toggle, and exports data to a Google Sheet."
- **Action:** Keep the conversation going until the AI gives you a full feature list and a logic map.

### 🎨 Step 2: The Design "Identity"

AI has a "default" look. To break out of it, you need to feed it visual data.

- **Inspiration:** Screenshot a UI you love from Dribbble or Pinterest and upload it.
- **Branding:** Upload your logo and hex codes for your colors.
- **Command:** "Apply this specific aesthetic to the entire dashboard. Use [Color A] for primary buttons and [Color B] for alerts."

### 🤖 Step 3: The Multi-Agent "War Room"

Don't ask one AI to do everything. Split the work into specialized agents inside AntiGravity.

- **Agent A (The Architect):** Handles the core features and database logic.
- **Agent B (The Designer):** Focuses strictly on CSS, animations, and "feel."
- **Agent C (The Debugger):** Runs the code in a live Chrome window to find and fix errors before you see them.

### ⚙️ Step 4: The Customization Layer

To make sure the app stays consistent as it grows, set "Project Rules."

- **Project Notes:** Tell the AI, "Always use Tailwind CSS" or "Never change the font size on mobile."
- **Rules:** Create a rule that says, "Every new feature must include an 'Undo' button." This ensures every future update follows your logic automatically.

---

### 🚢 The Finish Line: Deployment

Once the app is "Self-Tested" and "Self-Fixed" in the live window:

1. **Sync to GitHub:** Keep your code safe and version-controlled.
2. **Deploy to Vercel:** Click "Deploy" and your app is live on the internet with a real URL.
